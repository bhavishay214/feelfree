How to Build Good Relationships

DR. Sneha Kulhari
7 February | 8 min read

Good relationships—whether personal, professional, or social—are the foundation of a happy and fulfilling life. We are inherently social beings, and the quality of our relationships often defines the quality of our life. But building and maintaining strong relationships requires effort, emotional intelligence, and an understanding of what makes connections thrive. Whether you’re looking to improve friendships, family bonds, or work relationships, here are some key principles to help you nurture and sustain meaningful relationships.

1. Communication is Key At the heart of any strong relationship is effective communication. Being able to express your thoughts, feelings, and concerns openly is crucial to fostering trust and understanding. But communication is a two-way street—it's not just about speaking, but also about listening.
Active listening—truly hearing what someone is saying without planning your response in advance—shows the other person that you value their thoughts and feelings. When people feel heard, they are more likely to open up, deepening the connection.
Tip: Practice active listening by summarizing or reflecting back what the other person has said. This shows empathy and helps clarify misunderstandings.

2. Trust and Honesty Trust is the cornerstone of any good relationship. Without it, even the strongest connection can crumble. Trust is built over time through consistent actions and honesty. Being transparent, keeping promises, and owning up to mistakes are ways to cultivate trust.
Honesty, however, doesn’t mean being blunt or insensitive. Being honest while maintaining respect for the other person’s feelings fosters a relationship that is both truthful and compassionate.
Tip: Avoid the temptation to keep things bottled up or hide the truth to avoid conflict. Small lies or omissions can erode trust over time.

3. Respect Boundaries Healthy relationships are built on mutual respect, and part of that respect involves understanding and honoring each other’s boundaries. Everyone has limits—whether emotional, physical, or personal. Respecting these boundaries helps maintain balance in the relationship and prevents resentment or misunderstandings.
For example, in a professional setting, respecting a colleague’s work-life balance can strengthen your working relationship. In personal relationships, being mindful of emotional boundaries can ensure that both people feel safe and valued.
Tip: Don’t assume someone’s boundaries; ask questions and be observant. Always check in to make sure you're not overstepping.

4. Show Appreciation and Gratitude People thrive when they feel valued and appreciated. Simple acts of appreciation, whether it's a thank you, a compliment, or a thoughtful gesture, can go a long way in strengthening bonds. Taking the time to acknowledge what someone brings to the relationship fosters positivity and reinforces the connection.
It’s easy to take those closest to us for granted, especially in long-term relationships, but regularly expressing gratitude keeps the bond strong and shows that you don’t take the other person’s efforts for granted.
Tip: Make it a habit to acknowledge the small things. Whether it’s a colleague helping with a project or a friend lending a listening ear, a simple “thank you” can make all the difference.

5. Be Supportive Being there for someone when they need support is essential in building a lasting relationship. Whether it’s offering a shoulder to cry on, giving advice, or just being present during tough times, your willingness to support others strengthens the bond you share.
However, support isn’t just about being there during bad times—it’s also about celebrating the good times. Showing genuine happiness for someone’s successes and being their cheerleader is just as important as providing comfort when things go wrong.
Tip: When supporting someone, ask them what they need. Sometimes people just need to vent, while other times they may want advice. Clarifying this helps you provide the best form of support.

6. Practice Empathy Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of another person. It is a powerful tool in building good relationships because it allows you to connect with others on a deeper emotional level. When someone feels that you can relate to their experience, it builds trust, compassion, and a sense of mutual understanding.

Empathy requires you to put yourself in someone else’s shoes, to see things from their perspective, and to respond with care. This doesn’t mean you always have to agree, but it does mean you should acknowledge and validate their feelings.
Tip: Next time someone shares something with you, pause and reflect on what they might be feeling. Respond in a way that shows you understand their emotional state.

7. Make Time and Invest Effort Relationships, like anything else, require time and effort to thrive. Whether it’s family, friends, or colleagues, making time for the people who matter most is essential. In our busy lives, it’s easy to let relationships fall by the wayside, but consistent effort is what keeps them strong.
This might mean regular catch-ups with friends, weekly check-ins with family, or simply sending a thoughtful message to someone you haven’t spoken to in a while. Investing effort in small ways over time keeps relationships vibrant and healthy.
Tip: Schedule time to connect with people, even if it’s just a quick phone call or coffee. Regular contact helps maintain the bond and prevents the relationship from drifting.

8. Be Patient and Forgiving No relationship is perfect. There will be disagreements, misunderstandings, and times when things don’t go smoothly. The key to maintaining good relationships is patience and forgiveness. Holding onto grudges or harboring resentment only damages the connection over time.
Being patient with the other person’s flaws and being willing to forgive mistakes allows the relationship to move forward. Forgiveness doesn’t mean condoning bad behavior, but it does mean letting go of the negative emotions that can poison a relationship.
Tip: When conflicts arise, focus on resolving the issue rather than being right. Try to see things from the other person’s point of view and work toward a solution that benefits both of you.

9. Keep Growing Together Strong relationships are dynamic, not static. As people grow and evolve, so do their needs and desires. To maintain a healthy relationship, it’s important to grow together rather than apart. This means supporting each other’s personal development and adapting to the changes that naturally occur over time.
Whether it’s a personal relationship where interests evolve or a professional one where roles change, keeping an open mind and being willing to evolve together ensures that the relationship remains strong despite the passage of time.
Tip: Regularly check in with the other person about how the relationship is going. Discussing changes or challenges openly helps ensure that you grow together rather than drift apart.

Conclusion Building and maintaining good relationships is one of the most rewarding investments you can make. It requires effort, understanding, and emotional intelligence, but the benefits are lifelong. By communicating openly, showing empathy, being supportive, and nurturing trust, you can create relationships that bring happiness, growth, and fulfillment. Relationships are a two-way street, but by focusing on what you can bring to the table, you’ll naturally encourage stronger, deeper connections that stand the test of time.
